# Gas, Slips, and Swaps: A DeFi Vocabulary Guide 💸🌐

![DeFi Education](/img/education-banner.png)

If you're new to the world of decentralized finance (DeFi), it can feel like everyone is speaking a different language. Let's break down three of the most common terms you'll encounter while using OnlyFence to power your AI agent.

<!-- truncate -->

## 1. What is a DEX?

A **DEX (Decentralized Exchange)** is an online marketplace where users can trade cryptocurrencies directly with each other, without a giant corporation in the middle.

*   In a traditional exchange (like a stock market), you have to trust them with your money.
*   In a **DEX**, like **7K.ag** or **Cetus**, the trades happen via **Smart Contracts** on the blockchain. OnlyFence connects your AI agent to these DEXs automatically.

## 2. What are Gas Fees?

Think of **Gas Fees** as the fuel you pay for your car. In the blockchain world, a network (like SUI) is made up of thousands of computers verifying transactions. To pay for the energy and work these computers do, you pay a small fee in **SUI**.

*   Every swap, lend, or borrow action costs a tiny amount of Gas.
*   OnlyFence automatically calculates this for your agent, so you don't have to worry about the math.

## 3. What is Slippage?

Imagine you're buying something at an auction, but the price changes while you're shouting your bid. That's **Slippage**.

*   Because crypto prices move so fast, the price of a token might change between the moment your agent *requests* a trade and the moment it is *executed* on the blockchain.
*   Most DEXs allow you to set a **Slippage Tolerance** (e.g., 0.5%). If the price changes more than that, the trade is cancelled to protect you.

Understanding these terms is the key to mastering DeFi with OnlyFence. While your agent is doing the heavy lifting, knowing these core concepts helps you set the right guardrails for your strategy.
